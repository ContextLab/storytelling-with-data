## What makes a data story compelling?

<div class="definition-box" data-title="The anatomy of a data story">

A great data story combines three ingredients:

- **Data** — credible, relevant evidence that grounds the narrative
- **Narrative** — a structured storyline with a beginning, middle, and end
- **Visuals** — charts, figures, and images that make the data *feel* real

The key question to answer is the **"so what?" factor** — why should your audience care? If you just list what you did, you will end up with a boring story. Lead with the *why*, support with the *what*.

</div>

## Discussion: datasets and questions

<div class="example-box" data-title="Discussion prompt">

**What datasets interest you? What questions could they answer?**

Think about:
- Topics you are already curious about
- Data you encounter in your daily life (sports, music, social media, health)
- Public datasets (government, research, journalism)

Draw inspiration from your own life — it will be easiest to tell a story about something you are familiar with.

</div>

---

## Discussion: remixing stories

<div class="example-box" data-title="Discussion prompt">

**How can you remix an existing data story to tell something new?**

Consider:
- Taking a published visualization and asking a *different* question
- Combining two datasets that have not been connected before
- Updating an older analysis with new data
- Telling the same story from a different audience's perspective

</div>

## Workshop time

<div class="tip-box" data-title="Brainstorm with a partner">

1. **Pair up** with someone nearby
2. **Share your idea** — what dataset, what question, what story?
3. **Help each other sharpen the "so what?"** — why does this matter?
4. **Sketch a rough outline** — what are the key plot points?

Half-baked ideas are encouraged. You can also pitch multiple ideas and get help choosing.

</div>
